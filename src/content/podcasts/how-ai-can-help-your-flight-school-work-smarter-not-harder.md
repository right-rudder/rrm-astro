If you've ever felt like you're losing potential students because you can't respond fast enough, or you're spending more time wrestling with admin tasks than actually running your flight school, you're going to want to tune in to the next episode of The Aviation Business Podcast.

On **February 3 at 2 p.m. CST**, Right Rudder Marketing founder and CEO Tim Jedrek sits down with Otto Mand from [EZCopilot](https://ezcopilot.com/) to talk about something that's changing the game for flight schools: using AI and automation to grow more efficiently without losing the personal touch that makes your school special.

## What We'll Cover

This isn't going to be one of those tech-heavy conversations that leaves you more confused than when you started. We're keeping it practical and grounded in real-world flight school challenges.

You'll hear actionable ideas about:

**Catching and converting more leads.** When someone fills out your contact form at 9 p.m. on a Tuesday, what happens? If the answer is "they wait until someone checks email the next morning," you're already behind. We'll talk about how to speed up your response time without burning out your team.

**Creating consistency across your team.** Not every CFI is a natural salesperson, and that's okay. But when prospects get wildly different experiences depending on who answers the phone, you've got a problem. We'll explore how to standardize communication while keeping it personal.

**Building better student journeys.** From the moment someone discovers your school to the day they pass their checkride, there are dozens of touchpoints. We'll discuss how to make each one count and keep students engaged throughout their training.

**Getting your time back.** If you're a flight school owner who lives in your inbox, this one's for you. Learn how to automate the repetitive stuff so you can focus on leading your school and planning for growth.

## Meet Otto Mand

Otto created [EZCopilot](https://ezcopilot.com/) specifically to help aviation businesses work smarter. He's not interested in flashy tech for tech's sake. His whole approach is about removing friction from your daily operations so you and your team can do what you do best: teach people to fly.

Here's the thing: your competition isn't sleeping. While you're manually tracking down leads and trying to remember to follow up with that prospect from last week, other schools are building systems that do this automatically.
